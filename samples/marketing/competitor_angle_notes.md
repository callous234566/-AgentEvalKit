# Competitor Angle Notes

These notes are fictional and created only for product testing.

## Observed Market Patterns

- Many walking pad ads lead with weight loss or step count. This creates compliance risk and can feel generic.
- Smaller brands often show a treadmill under a standing desk, but they rarely explain noise level or apartment storage.
- Stronger review language focuses on convenience: "I use it during email" and "I forgot it was there after a week."
- The most believable videos show normal work contexts: calendar blocks, headphones, calls, and a small apartment.

## White Space

1. Calendar-based movement: frame the product as a workday workflow tool, not gym equipment.
2. Quiet proof: show call audio, shared apartment context, or a noise comparison.
3. Tiny habit: make the first step feel easy and non-athletic.
4. Storage reveal: show the treadmill disappearing behind common furniture.

## Risks

- Avoid medical or guaranteed weight-loss claims.
- Avoid before/after body imagery.
- Avoid implying walking while working is suitable for every job or every person.
